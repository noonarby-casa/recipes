## Instructions

1. Prepare the rice so that it is done at the same time as the butter chickpeas.
2. Heat a large skillet over medium heat, add butter ({{< qty "4 tablespoons" >}}) and let melt.
3. Add cumin powder ({{< qty "1/4 teaspoon" >}}) and toast for about {{< timer "10 seconds" >}}, then stir in the chopped onion ({{< qty "1 1/2 cups" >}}), crushed garlic ({{< qty "2 large cloves" >}}), ginger ({{< qty "2 teaspoons" >}}), and salt ({{< qty "1/2 teaspoon" >}}). Cook, stirring often, until the onions get a golden brown color ({{< timer "4-6 minutes" >}}).
4. Add Kasoori methi ({{< qty "2 tablespoons" >}}), coriander powder ({{< qty "2 1/2 teaspoons" >}}), cayenne pepper powder ({{< qty "1/2 teaspoon" >}}), and garam masala powder ({{< qty "1/4 teaspoon" >}}). Cook for {{< timer "3 minutes" >}}, stirring constantly, until the spices are toasted well (don't let them burn).
5. Add the tomato sauce ({{< qty "1 can" >}}), cashew powder {{< qty "3 tablespoons" >}}, chickpeas ({{< qty "2 cans" >}}), and water ({{< qty "1/2 cup" >}}). Stir well, reduce the heat to low, cover, and cook for {{< timer "10 minutes" >}} (stirring every {{< timer "3-4 minutes" >}} to make sure that it does not burn on the bottom).
6. Uncover, stir in the heavy cream ({{< qty "1/2 cup" >}}), maple syrup ({{< qty "2 tablespoons" >}}), and the remaining salt ({{< qty "1/2 teaspoon" >}}). Let simmer for about {{< timer "2 minutes" >}} (mix in a splash of water if the curry looks too thick).
7. Remove from the heat and let the curry rest for a few minutes before serving.
