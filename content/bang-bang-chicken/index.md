## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 220°C (425°F). Line a baking tray with parchment paper.
2. Make the bang bang sauce: Combine the mayonnaise ({{< qty "1 cup" >}}), sweet chilli sauce ({{< qty "1/4 cup" >}}), sriracha ({{< qty "1 tablespoon" >}}), honey ({{< qty "1 tablespoon" >}}), and lime juice ({{< qty "1 tablespoon" >}}) in a small bowl and set aside. Reserve one-quarter of the sauce in a separate bowl to brush over the raw chicken prior to baking.
3. Prep the chicken: Place the chicken ({{< qty "1 1/3 pounds" >}}) on a chopping board with a sheet of baking paper over the top. Use a mallet or rolling pin to pound the chicken to an even thickness. Cut each piece in half vertically.
4. Marinate the chicken: Place the chicken in a large shallow bowl and add the sweet paprika ({{< qty "1 teaspoon" >}}), onion powder ({{< qty "1 teaspoon" >}}), black pepper ({{< qty "1/4 teaspoon" >}}), tamari ({{< qty "1 tablespoon" >}}), brown sugar ({{< qty "1 tablespoon" >}}), and olive oil ({{< qty "1 tablespoon" >}}). Using tongs, toss the chicken to coat it evenly in the marinade.
5. Cook the chicken: Arrange the chicken on the prepared baking tray so that it is not overlapping. If the chicken is overcrowded in the tray, it will steam rather than caramelized.
6. Using a pastry brush, brush the reserved bang bang sauce (about {{< qty "1/4 cup" >}}) all over the tops of the chicken pieces.
7. Bake the chicken for {{< timer "25-30 minutes" >}} or until golden and cooked through.
8. Serve: Serve with steamed rice, red cabbage ({{< qty "75 grams" >}}), carrots ({{< qty "2" >}}), cucumber ({{< qty "1" >}}), and spring onions ({{< qty "1" >}}). Drizzle with the remaining bang bang sauce and sprinkle with spring onions.
