## Instructions

1. In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey ({{< qty "1 pound" >}}), egg ({{< qty "1" >}}), cracker crumbs ({{< qty "1 cup" >}}), garlic ({{< qty "2 cloves" >}}), ginger ({{< qty "1 teaspoon" >}}), coconut aminos ({{< qty "1.5 tablespoons" >}}), red curry paste ({{< qty "1 tablespoon" >}}), and sea salt and black pepper to taste. Mix well with a spatula or large fork until fully combined.
2. Place the bowl in the fridge to chill for {{< timer "15-30 minutes" >}}.
3. Preheat oven to 400°F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
4. Roll the turkey mixture into about 1-inch balls and arrange on the parchment paper.
5. Bake for {{< timer "18 minutes" >}} (or until the meatballs are cooked through and no longer pink inside).
6. While the meatballs bake, make the coconut curry: In a large skillet, warm the oil ({{< qty "2 tablespoons" >}}), sliced red bell pepper ({{< qty "1" >}}), and diced onion ({{< qty "1 small" >}}) over medium heat. Cook for {{< timer "5-7 minutes" >}} or until golden.
7. Add the red curry paste ({{< qty "2 tablespoons" >}}), coconut milk ({{< qty "1 can" >}}), and coconut aminos ({{< qty "1 tablespoon" >}}). Mix well over medium-low heat.
8. Allow the coconut curry to simmer for a bit while the meatballs finish baking, then add the cooked meatballs to the coconut curry. Simmer together for {{< timer "30-60 minutes" >}} if possible so the flavors combine.
9. Garnish with cilantro and serve warm over your favorite rice, stir-fry noodles, or zucchini noodles. Enjoy!
